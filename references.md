# Model Parameter References

This document lists the sources for the key parameters used in the Health Economic Analysis models.

## 1. HPV Vaccination
*   **Source:** PHARMAC & Ministry of Health (2017). "HPV vaccination funding decision".
*   **Key Findings:** Cost-effectiveness estimated at ~$18,800/QALY for girls. Funded for ages 9-26.
*   **URL:** [PHARMAC Decision](https://www.pharmac.govt.nz/news-and-resources/consultations-and-decisions/decision-to-widen-access-to-hpv-vaccine)

## 2. Smoking Cessation
*   **Source:** Ministry of Health (2021). "New Zealand Guidelines for Helping People to Stop Smoking".
*   **Key Findings:** Highly cost-effective; break-even point for interventions is ~$13,000 - $29,000 per quitter.
*   **URL:** [MoH Guidelines](https://www.health.govt.nz/publication/new-zealand-guidelines-helping-people-stop-smoking)

## 3. Hepatitis C Therapy (DAAs)
*   **Source:** PHARMAC (2019). "Proposal to widen access to funded hepatitis C treatments".
*   **Key Findings:** ICER consistently <$100,000/QALY. Generic DAA treatment costs ~$2,000 - $4,000.
*   **URL:** [PHARMAC Proposal](https://pharmac.govt.nz/news-and-resources/consultations-and-decisions/decision-to-widen-access-to-funded-hepatitis-c-treatments)

## 4. Childhood Obesity Prevention
*   **Source:** Health Research Council & Ministry of Health (2020). "Cost-effectiveness of childhood obesity interventions".
*   **Key Findings:** School-based programs and potential sugar taxes are most cost-effective. Sugar tax could save $55 for every $1 spent.
*   **URL:** [HRC Research Repository](https://www.hrc.govt.nz/resources/research-repository/cost-effectiveness-childhood-obesity-interventions)

## 5. Housing Insulation
*   **Source:** Motu Economic and Public Policy Research. "Cost Benefit Analysis of the Warm Up New Zealand: Heat Smart Programme".
*   **Key Findings:** Benefit-Cost Ratio ~4:1. 99% of benefits are health-related (reduced respiratory illness).
*   **URL:** [Motu Report](https://www.motu.nz/our-work/urban-and-regional/housing/cost-benefit-analysis-of-the-warm-up-new-zealand-heat-smart-programme/)
